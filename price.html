FAQ


Which subscription is right for me?

Kosai recommends everyone starts on Enthusiast tier at $0 a month.  ​This is effectively a free trial and you can stay on it forever.  Even if you are using SOSSHub in commercial settings, you only have to upgrade immediately prior to moving test code into production for the first time, or immediately prior to shipping software to customers.  Feel free to prototype while on the Enthusiast tier as long as you respect the server limit.


What counts as commercial use?

Commercial use is any production use of SOSSHub content on any computer owned by any legal entity (as opposed to a natural person), even a sole proprietorship.​


How does Kosai define resale?

Resale is when any SOSSHub content is contained within another piece of software that ​is provided by a legal entity to any other non-affiliated legal entity, even if it is done free of charge.


How does Kosai define a package subscription?

The combination of a package name and a version number counts as one package subscription.  Your subscription includes continuous monitoring and unlimited patching for unlimited CVEs of the package/version combo for the term of your contract.


How does Kosai define a server?

Any single collection of computer hardware serving data, services or resources to clients over a network.  Client computers do not count as servers and all SOSSHub users receive unlimited clients.  Infinite VMs running on infinite cores on infinite chips on infinite boards on one motherboard is one server.


What if I can't find a package I am interested in?

Within the SOSSHub platform, there is an unlimited package request feature available to Enterprise and Embedded tier users.  Enthusiast tier users have access to a limited number of requests.  Submit your request, go for a walk, and it should be ready by the time you get back.​


Does 100% of my Enthusiast tier donation really go to the community?

Technically, no.  Kosai isn't a bank, so transaction fees​ come out of the total.  Outside of that, yes, all of the rest does actually go to the community.  Kosai also does its best to reduce transaction fees on our end, and you can do the same by paying via ETF instead of credit card if you like.


Can I control where my donation goes?

Absolutely!  Subscribe to packages you want to donate to, and unsubscribe from packages you no longer want to donate to.  Kosai will handle the rest.


How is my donation distributed to the community?

Every month, Kosai divides your donation by the number of packages you are subscribed to at that time, and distributes shares to each team.  If you are subscribed to no packages, Kosai​ will donate your contribution to an appropriate open source foundation.


What happens if I change my donation mid-month?

If you increase your donation, Kosai will process the new charge immediately with an appropriate credit applied against it, and reset your monthly renewal date.  As we have already distributed your donation​ to the community, if you decrease your donation, Kosai will process the new amount on your next monthly renewal date.


What happens if I need to change the amount of package subs I have purchased mid-year?

If you increase your subscription, Kosai will prorate ​the cost over the remainder of your current annual term.  If you decrease your subscription, Kosai will process the new amount on your next annual renewal date.  Kosai does not issue partial refunds in this scenario.


How do I donate as a maintainer?

You have two options.  Either start my claiming your project, then change your donation amount within the SOSSHub platform.  Or start with an Enthusiast subscription, then claim your project ​within the SOSSHub platform.  Please note that for security reasons, Kosai does not allow maintainers to donate to themselves.  If you subscribe to your own package, it will be automatically ignored when calculating distribution of your specific donation.  If you only subscribe to your own packages, Kosai treats that as zero package subs.
